The Fountain of Youth

She found the Fountain of Youth, it seems,
In the later years of her life.
She is the same as she was under all that hair,
But now is a younger and more beautiful wife.

Her locks were thinning as the years go by,
A solution was needed before it was all gone.
She picked up the phone book and scanned it a bit,
And drove to the Mane Door Salon.

There presided Nancee, the Great Artist of Hair,
Who weaved her spell on the lass.
And when she was done, the years melted away,
Her client looked much younger with such class.

So if you find yourself in a hole, losing your hair,
Do not just continue to dig.
Take yourself down to the Mane Door Salon,
And get yourself fitted with a wig!

The world become brighter, the birds applaud from the trees,
The men turn their heads and stare.
And the women are buzzing amongst themselves,
About how did she grow all that hair?

So when she is questioned about who did her hair,
And do you think they can do the same with mine?
And she just smiles so gently and all she says is:
The Mane Door Salon turned me out so fine.

Jay Grooters
